HOW TO TEST
—
1
What does the AC actually do?
▶
AC does not create cold. It moves heat. It takes heat from inside your room and dumps it outside. That's it. The refrigerant is the carrier — it picks up heat inside and drops it outside. Cold is just what's left when heat is removed.
2
Why does low gas cause icing on the pipe?
▶
Low refrigerant = low pressure in evaporator = lower boiling point = coil gets too cold = moisture in air freezes on it. Ice builds up, blocks airflow completely. Now nothing works. Fix: find the leak first. Never just top up. Topping up without fixing the leak = same problem in 2 weeks.
3
Why does high ambient temperature affect the AC so much?
▶
The condenser dumps heat to outside air. At 48°C outside, it is much harder to dump heat than at 25°C. The compressor works harder, pressure rises, efficiency drops. UAE summer = AC working at absolute maximum capacity every day. This is why capacitors, contactors, and fan motors fail faster here than anywhere else.
4
Burnt contactor relay coil — what does it mean?
▶
The contactor coil pulls in the contacts to start the compressor. If the compressor is internally shorted it draws massive current → contactor overheats → coil burns. A burnt contactor coil is a symptom, not the root cause. Replace contactor, then check compressor amps immediately on startup. If amps spike above RLA = compressor is dying. If amps are normal = contactor just aged out.
5
Compressor won't start — exact test sequence
▶
Do in this order:

1. Check capacitor first (C/HERM terminal) — most common cause
2. Check contactor contacts for pitting or burning
3. Measure supply voltage at compressor terminals
4. Megger test compressor windings (should be >1MΩ to earth)
5. Check compressor winding resistance (R-S, R-C, S-C) — all should be balanced

If windings show short to earth → compressor dead. Do not replace capacitor and leave. Client will call back tomorrow.

HOW TO TEST
—
1
What does a capacitor actually do?
▶
A motor needs a strong kick to start spinning from zero. The capacitor stores electrical charge and releases it all at once — like a spring being released. This creates a second phase of power that makes the motor torque up and start. Without it, the motor tries to start, hums, draws huge current, and either trips the MCB or burns the winding.
2
Why do UAE capacitors fail so fast?
▶
Heat is the enemy of capacitors. Every 10°C above rated temperature halves the capacitor lifespan. Outdoor unit panels in UAE reach 70–80°C in summer. A capacitor rated for 10 years in Europe lasts 2–3 years here. This is the single most common part failure in UAE AC systems. Always carry spares.
3
Can I replace just one section of a dual capacitor?
▶
No. Dual run capacitors are one sealed unit. If one section fails — replace the whole capacitor. You can temporarily use two separate single capacitors if you cannot get the exact dual type, but match the µF ratings exactly. Never oversize a capacitor — higher µF draws higher current and can burn the motor winding.

1
What is R22 and why is it being phased out?
▶
R22 (Freon) is an old refrigerant that damages the ozone layer. It has been banned for new equipment since 2015 in most countries. In UAE many older villas (pre-2010) still run R22 systems. You cannot legally purchase new R22 in UAE — only recycled R22 from recovery cylinders.
2
R22 pressure readings — what is normal?
▶
At 35°C ambient:
Suction: 60–75 PSI
Discharge: 220–260 PSI

At 45°C ambient (UAE summer):
Suction: 65–80 PSI
Discharge: 250–300 PSI

Much lower pressures than R410A. Do not confuse systems. Wrong refrigerant = destroyed compressor.
3
Should I convert an R22 system to R410A?
▶
No. R410A runs at much higher pressures than R22. Old R22 copper piping and components cannot handle R410A pressure — they will fail or leak. If an R22 system needs full recharge — recommend full system replacement to client. Topping up old R22 with recycled gas buys time, nothing more.

WHAT TO DO
—
1
What is the difference between MCB and RCBO?
▶
MCB (Miniature Circuit Breaker) — protects against overload and short circuit only. It measures current.

RCBO (Residual Current Breaker with Overload) — does everything an MCB does PLUS detects earth faults as small as 30mA. It detects current leaking to earth — which can kill a person.

In UAE, bathrooms and outdoor circuits should have RCBO protection by regulation.
2
How to identify circuits in an unlabelled DB
▶
Switch off all MCBs except main. Turn on first sub-MCB. Walk through villa noting what came on. Label it. Repeat for each MCB. Always do this with someone else present — one person at DB, one walking the villa communicating by phone. Never leave a client's DB unlabelled after you've identified circuits. Use a marker and tape. 10 minutes now saves 2 hours next time.
3
Single phase vs two-way switch wiring
▶
Single switch: Live → switch → lamp → neutral. Simple.

Two-way switch (staircase): Two switches control one light. Live goes to common (C) of switch 1. Strappers (L1, L2) connect both switches. Neutral goes direct to lamp. Output from switch 2 common goes to lamp live.

Both switches must be two-way type — they have 3 terminals (C, L1, L2) not 2.

Fault: light works from one switch only = one switch is single-way type. Replace it with two-way.

FAULT FINDING
—
1
Why does the villa have two tanks?
▶
DEWA supply pressure varies — it can drop or stop completely during peak demand. The ground tank provides storage buffer. The roof tank provides constant gravity pressure to the villa regardless of what DEWA supply is doing at any moment. Without the roof tank, every drop in DEWA pressure would cause fluctuating water pressure in the villa.
2
What is a float switch and how does it work?
▶
A float switch is a simple on/off switch attached to a floating ball. When water level is low — float drops — contacts close — pump switches on. When water level is high — float rises — contacts open — pump switches off.

Stuck float (tangled on fitting) = pump runs all day = wasted electricity + possible overflow. Check float can move freely.
3
No water in villa — exact fault finding sequence
▶
Start at the roof — work down:

1. Check roof tank — is it empty or full?
2. If EMPTY → go to ground tank, check level
3. Ground tank EMPTY → DEWA supply issue or inlet valve closed
4. Ground tank FULL → pump fault (MCB, motor, float switch, NRV)
5. Roof tank FULL → gravity feed pipe blocked or valve closed

Never start at the taps. Always start at the source and work toward the taps.

Light faults — bulbs, drivers, switches
LED · Halogen GU10 · Two-way wiring
▶

→ Check MCB first. Then driver. Then bulb. In that order, every time.

1
LED downlight vs GU10 halogen — what's the difference?
▶
LED downlight: Has a separate LED driver (small black box) that converts 220V to low DC. The LED strip/module itself runs at 12V, 24V, or 36V DC. When the light fails — it is usually the driver that fails, not the LED. Replace driver first. AED 15–30.

GU10 halogen: Runs directly on 220V. No driver needed. Two-pin twist-and-lock fitting. When it fails — just the bulb. AED 5 each. But: UAE ceiling temperatures reach 65°C in summer — GU10 bulbs fail every 3–6 months. Consider recommending LED GU10 replacement (AED 15) — lasts 5 years.
2
LED driver keeps failing — why?
▶
Heat kills LED drivers. They are rated at 40–60°C maximum. UAE ceiling voids in summer reach 70°C+. The driver overheats, thermal protection trips it, it fails.

Solutions:
1. Use a higher-rated driver (check temperature specification on box)
2. Ensure ceiling void has some ventilation
3. Do not use a higher wattage driver than needed — excess heat

If same driver fails in same fitting within 6 months — temperature problem, not driver quality problem.
3
Two-way switch wiring — how to test which wire is which
▶
Wires at switch box: Live (L), Strapper 1 (S1), Strapper 2 (S2), possibly Neutral (N) and Earth (E).

To test: Use a tester screwdriver. With switch box open and circuit live, the Live wire will glow the tester. The two strappers will only glow depending on switch position.

Common fault: Light works from Switch 1 only, not Switch 2 → Switch 2 is single-way type. It has only 2 terminals. Replace with two-way type (3 terminals: C, L1, L2).

Always switch off MCB before working inside switch boxes. Test dead with tester before touching wires.
4
UK socket wiring — UAE standard
▶
UAE uses UK-standard 13A sockets (BS 1363). Three rectangular pins.

Wiring inside socket:
— Live (L) → Brown wire → Bottom right terminal
— Neutral (N) → Blue wire → Bottom left terminal
— Earth (E) → Green/Yellow wire → Top terminal

The earth pin is the longest — it opens the safety shutters inside the socket before live and neutral connect.

Never leave earth wire disconnected. In UAE humidity, unearthed metal appliances can give serious shock.

R410A / R32 pressure quick reference
UAE ambient temps · normal ranges · fault signs
▶

→ Use the Pressure Calculator in Tools for exact readings. This is the quick reference.

1
R410A normal pressures — UAE summer
▶
At 35°C ambient:
Suction (low side): 110–130 PSI
Discharge (high side): 320–380 PSI

At 45°C ambient:
Suction: 120–145 PSI
Discharge: 380–450 PSI

At 50°C ambient (peak UAE):
Suction: 130–155 PSI
Discharge: 420–500 PSI

High discharge PSI at high ambient is NORMAL. Do not condemn compressor based on high-side reading alone in UAE summer.
2
R32 — becoming more common, different pressures
▶
R32 is replacing R410A in newer systems (post-2020). It is more efficient but runs at slightly higher pressures.

At 45°C ambient:
Suction: 130–155 PSI
Discharge: 400–480 PSI

R32 is mildly flammable (A2L classification). Do not use open flame near R32 systems during service. Ensure ventilation when recovering or charging.

Check the nameplate on the outdoor unit — it always states the refrigerant type.
3
What low suction pressure actually means
▶
Suction below 80 PSI (R410A at 45°C) = one of these:

1. Low refrigerant — most common. Find leak. Do not just top up.
2. Blocked expansion device — suction very low, discharge normal or high. Superheat very high.
3. Dirty evaporator coil — restricted airflow starves evaporator. Check filter and coil.
4. Indoor fan not running — same effect as dirty coil.

Suction pipe iced = low refrigerant or restricted airflow. Stop unit, find cause before restarting.
